## ACTION BY UNANIMOUS WRITTEN CONSENT

The undersigned, being all of the members of the Board of Directors of **Mise, Inc.**, a Delaware corporation (the "**Corporation**"), hereby adopt the following resolutions by unanimous written consent in accordance with Section 141(f) of the Delaware General Corporation Law and Article IV, Section 8 of the Corporation's Bylaws, effective as of:

**Effective Date:** /TextEffectiveDate/

---

## 1. RATIFICATION OF CERTIFICATE OF INCORPORATION

**RESOLVED**, that the Certificate of Incorporation of the Corporation, as filed with the Secretary of State of the State of Delaware on November 19, 2025 (File Number 10409178, SR 20254615180), be and hereby is ratified, confirmed, and approved in all respects.

---

## 2. ADOPTION OF BYLAWS; SUPERSESSION OF PRIOR BYLAWS

**RESOLVED**, that the Corporate Bylaws of Mise, Inc. executed via DocuSign (Envelope ID: 562323BB-9CA0-461F-A3CB-BB1077436325) on January 22, 2026, attached hereto as **Exhibit A**, be and hereby are adopted as the Bylaws of the Corporation, effective as of January 22, 2026.

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the prior version of the Corporate Bylaws adopted on November 21, 2025 (which listed a share allocation of 6,000,000 shares to Jonathan Flaig and 4,000,000 shares to Austin Miett) is hereby formally **superseded and replaced in its entirety** by the Bylaws adopted herein. To the extent any provision of the prior bylaws conflicts with the Bylaws adopted herein or with the Shareholder Agreement dated January 22, 2026, the Bylaws adopted herein and the Shareholder Agreement shall control.

---

## 3. ELECTION OF OFFICERS

**RESOLVED**, that the following persons are hereby elected to serve in the offices set forth opposite their respective names, effective as of November 19, 2025, to serve until their successors are duly elected and qualified or until their earlier resignation or removal:

| Office | Name |
|--------|------|
| President and Chief Executive Officer | Jonathan Flaig |
| Secretary and Treasurer | Austin Miett |

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the officers so elected shall have such powers and duties as are set forth in the Bylaws of the Corporation, and as may be assigned from time to time by the Board of Directors.

---

## 4. AUTHORIZATION OF ISSUANCE OF SHARES

**RESOLVED**, that the Corporation is authorized to issue up to **10,000,000 shares** of Common Stock, par value $0.0001 per share, as set forth in the Certificate of Incorporation.

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the officers of the Corporation are hereby authorized and directed to issue shares of Common Stock to the following persons in the amounts set forth below, in exchange for the consideration indicated:

| Name | Number of Shares | Percentage | Consideration |
|------|------------------|------------|---------------|
| Jonathan Flaig | 7,000,000 | 70% | Services rendered to the Corporation, assignment of intellectual property pursuant to the Intellectual Property Assignment Agreement, and other good and valuable consideration |
| Austin Miett | 3,000,000 | 30% | Services rendered to the Corporation, assignment of intellectual property pursuant to the Intellectual Property Assignment Agreement, and other good and valuable consideration |

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that upon receipt of such consideration, the shares so issued shall be validly issued, fully paid, and non-assessable, and shall be fully vested and non-forfeitable as set forth in the Shareholder Agreement dated January 22, 2026.

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the share allocations set forth herein are consistent with and supersede any prior share allocation, including the allocation of 6,000,000 and 4,000,000 shares set forth in the Corporate Bylaws dated November 21, 2025, which is hereby formally corrected.

---

## 5. RATIFICATION OF SHAREHOLDER AGREEMENT

**RESOLVED**, that the Shareholder Agreement of Mise, Inc. dated January 22, 2026, executed via DocuSign (Envelope ID: 562323BB-9CA0-461F-A3CB-BB1077436325) by Jonathan Flaig and Austin Miett, be and hereby is ratified, confirmed, and approved in all respects, including without limitation the provisions relating to share ownership (7,000,000 shares / 3,000,000 shares), transfer restrictions, right of first refusal, co-sale rights, governance, confidentiality, intellectual property, and dispute resolution.

---

## 6. APPROVAL OF FOUNDER INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY ASSIGNMENTS

**RESOLVED**, that the Corporation is hereby authorized to enter into Intellectual Property Assignment Agreements with each of Jonathan Flaig and Austin Miett, substantially in the forms presented to the Board, pursuant to which each founder will irrevocably assign to the Corporation all right, title, and interest in and to all intellectual property relating to the Corporation's business, including intellectual property conceived, created, or developed prior to the Corporation's date of incorporation.

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the officers of the Corporation are hereby authorized and directed to execute such Intellectual Property Assignment Agreements and to take all actions necessary to effectuate and perfect such assignments, including filing applications for patents, copyrights, trademarks, and other intellectual property protections as may be appropriate.

---

## 7. STOCK CERTIFICATES; UNCERTIFICATED SHARES

**RESOLVED**, that pursuant to Section 158 of the Delaware General Corporation Law and Article VI, Section 1 of the Corporation's Bylaws, all shares of Common Stock of the Corporation shall be **uncertificated shares**. The Corporation shall not be required to issue stock certificates.

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the officers of the Corporation are hereby authorized to cause to be entered in the stock ledger of the Corporation the names and addresses of the stockholders, the number of shares held by each stockholder, the date of issuance, and such other information as may be required by law.

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the Corporation shall, upon request by any stockholder, provide a written statement of the information required by Section 151(f) of the Delaware General Corporation Law regarding the rights, preferences, and limitations of the shares held by such stockholder.

---

## 8. ACCOUNTING YEAR

**RESOLVED**, that the fiscal year of the Corporation shall be the calendar year, ending on **December 31** of each year.

## 13. TAX AND REGULATORY COMPLIANCE

**RESOLVED**, that the officers of the Corporation are hereby authorized and directed to file all required federal, state, and local tax returns, including without limitation:

> (a) IRS Form 1120 (U.S. Corporation Income Tax Return) for the short tax year ending December 31, 2025, due April 15, 2026;
>
> (b) IRS Form 941 (Employer's Quarterly Federal Tax Return) as required;
>
> (c) IRS Form 940 (Employer's Annual Federal Unemployment Tax Return) as required;
>
> (d) Delaware Annual Franchise Tax Report, due March 1 of each year; and
>
> (e) such other filings as may be required by applicable law.

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the officers are authorized to engage such accountants, tax preparers, and other professionals as they may deem necessary or appropriate to prepare and file such returns.

## 14. QUALIFICATION IN OTHER JURISDICTIONS

**RESOLVED**, that the officers of the Corporation are hereby authorized to take such actions and execute such documents as may be necessary or appropriate to qualify the Corporation to do business as a foreign corporation in the State of Florida, and in any other state or jurisdiction where such qualification is required by reason of the Corporation's business activities.

---

## 15. INDEMNIFICATION AND INSURANCE

**RESOLVED**, that the Corporation shall indemnify and hold harmless each director and officer of the Corporation, and each person who may have served at the Corporation's request as a director or officer of another corporation, to the fullest extent permitted by the Delaware General Corporation Law, as it now exists or may hereafter be amended.

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the Corporation shall pay or reimburse the reasonable expenses incurred by any director or officer in advance of the final disposition of any action, suit, or proceeding, upon receipt of an undertaking by or on behalf of such person to repay such amount if it shall ultimately be determined that such person is not entitled to be indemnified by the Corporation.

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the officers of the Corporation are hereby authorized to procure **Directors and Officers (D&O) liability insurance** in such amounts and on such terms as they deem reasonably necessary to protect the directors and officers of the Corporation, and to pay the premiums therefor from the Corporation's funds.
