# Mise, AGI, and Defensibility

**A Strategic Briefing for 2026–2035**

---

## Executive Framing

The core fear driving this analysis was simple and existential:

*If AGI becomes powerful enough to run a restaurant end-to-end, does Mise still need to exist?*

After examining timelines, architectures, intelligence theory, economic constraints, and platform risk, the conclusion is clear:

**Mise is not a wrapper. It is a prosthesis.**

And that distinction is the difference between fragility and durability in an AGI world.

---

## 1. The State of AGI (2026 Reality Check)

**Timeline uncertainty is real — but irrelevant to Mise's survival**

- **Bullish camp:** AGI by 2028–2030 (compute-driven, 50% probability by 2028)
- **Academic skeptics:** Median prediction collapsing from ~2060 to ~2040
- **"Already here" camp:** Frontier models already exceed humans on most standardized competence tests

**Key insight:**
Whether AGI arrives fast, slow, or stalls entirely, Mise's core value proposition does not disappear.

---

## 2. The Most Important Technical Insight: Jagged Intelligence

**Intelligence is not a single number**

According to arXiv 2510.18212, intelligence is a profile (CHC theory), not a scalar.

Current frontier models in 2026 are:

**Superhuman in:**

- Crystallized knowledge (facts, vocabulary)
- Fluid reasoning (novel problem solving)

**Weak in:**

- Long-term episodic memory
- Persistent context
- Embodied, physical-world understanding

This jaggedness is architectural, not a temporary data problem.

---

## 3. What This Means in a Restaurant (The Omnibot Test)

A hypothetical god-tier model ("Omnibot") can:

- Recite every cocktail recipe ever
- Solve complex reasoning problems
- Generate perfect plans

But it cannot:

- Remember which keg tap sticks
- Track informal promises
- Understand "the usual" on a Tuesday
- Retain one-off, local, human context over time

**Humans outperform AGI precisely where restaurants actually operate.**

---

## 4. Mise's True Identity (The Reframe)

**Mise is not competing with AGI.**

Mise:

- Fills the valleys in AGI's jagged intelligence profile
- Acts as an external memory, context, and continuity layer
- Serves as the operational brain for the restaurant

**Mise is an external hard drive for reality.**

As long as intelligence remains jagged, Mise remains necessary.

---

## 5. Agency Matters More Than Intelligence

Using OpenAI's Five Levels of AI:

1. Chatbots (passive)
2. Reasoners (think before answering)
3. Agents (execute actions over time)
4. Innovators
5. Organizations

**Mise is explicitly Level 3**

- Payroll agent
- Inventory agent
- Ordering agent
- Domain-specific action systems

This is not "ChatGPT for restaurants." That would be a dead wrapper.

---

## 6. The Transrouter: The Architectural Keystone

The transrouter is the most important technical concept in the entire system.

**What it solves:**

- Human speech is high-entropy
- Backend systems require zero-entropy
- One spoken sentence often contains multiple intents

**Example:**

*"We're almost out of lemons — add it to the order — and tell Ryan to clock out."*

The transrouter:

- Parses messy speech
- Separates intents
- Routes actions to the correct agents
- Maintains state and safety

**This is infrastructure, not prompting.**

Mise is building a Level-3 containment vessel that allows agentic systems to operate safely in the real world.

---

## 7. The Wrapper Risk — and Why Mise Avoids It

**What kills wrappers:**

- Thin UIs over platform models
- Platform owners ship the same feature
- Entire startup categories go to zero overnight

**Mise's moat is not the model**

It is entropy conversion:

- Converts high-entropy human speech
- Into zero-entropy, bank-safe execution

**The Approval JSON Contract:**

- AI must express intent as rigid, executable code
- Human approval gate before execution
- This is essential where money, payroll, and compliance are involved

**Big tech fails here because:**

- They don't know local meaning
- They don't learn "the usual"
- They don't own the workflow

Mise captures data that does not exist anywhere else:

- Pre-shift and post-shift audio
- Internal monologue of the business
- Tone, stress, friction, intent

**Google has:**

- Menus

**Mise has:**

- The soul of the shift

At scale, this becomes the most valuable hospitality operations dataset in existence.

## 9. The Economic Argument (Why Restaurants Pay)

From Stanford's AI and Our Economic Future:

- Any system is constrained by its weakest link
- In restaurants, the weakest link is manager cognitive capacity

**Managers are trapped doing admin instead of hospitality.**

Mise:

- Automates the cognitive bottleneck
- Creates silicon staff
- Decouples revenue growth from headcount

**Macro tailwind:**

- Structural labor shortage
- AI in food & beverage projected at $263B by 2034

---

## 10. Strategic Playbook (Immediate Actions)

**1. Become Load-Bearing**

- Deep integrations
- Mise is the only way data gets updated
- High switching costs

**2. Sell Trust, Not Autonomy**

- Draft-and-verify
- Approval button as a feature
- "Kill switch as a service"

**3. Stay Model-Agnostic**

- Never marry a provider
- Abstract the LLM layer
- Run core logic locally where possible

**4. Prepare for the Agent Economy**

- Expose clean APIs
- Enable agent-to-agent negotiation
- Mise becomes an economic actor

## 11. Future Trajectory: From Reactive to Proactive

**The real scaling moment:**

- Mise moves from recording → predicting → optimizing
- One intelligence coordinating 50+ locations
- Administrative backend OS for hospitality

This is how a product becomes a platform.

**If AGI arrives fast:**
Mise is the essential bridge

**If AGI stalls:**

Hospitality is cultural, not abstract. This is not a problem big tech understands deeply.
