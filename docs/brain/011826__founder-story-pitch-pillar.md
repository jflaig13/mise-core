### The Positioning

## Why Now (The Timing Advantage)

Three things converged in 2024-2025:

### 1. Whisper Changed Everything
OpenAI's Whisper model made speech-to-text essentially free and accurate. Before Whisper, voice transcription was expensive, inaccurate, and required cloud dependencies. Now it runs locally with 95%+ accuracy.

### 2. LLMs Can Parse Messy Human Speech
Five years ago, turning "Thursday PM, utility was John, Kevin sixty-five oh one, food sales two ninety-five" into structured JSON required brittle regex and prayer. Now Claude can understand context, infer meaning, and handle variations.

### 3. The Labor Crisis Peaked
- Restaurant labor costs up 30% since 2019
- Minimum wage increases continuing across states
- Worker shortage persists post-COVID
- Owners working 70+ hour weeks doing admin themselves
- They can't afford to hire back-office help
- They're doing payroll, inventory, scheduling themselves
- They're exhausted and looking for relief

**All three had to converge.** Five years ago, the tech wasn't ready. Five years from now, someone else might have done it. **Right now is the window.**

## Why Small Restaurants (The Strategic Wedge)

### The Opportunity

- 500,000+ owner-operators doing their own admin
- Underserved by enterprise-focused vendors
- Pain is acute (they're drowning)
- Switching cost is low (not locked into enterprise contracts)
- Word of mouth is strong (restaurant owners know each other)

### Why Big Players Ignore Them

- Low ARPU (single location vs. 20-location chain)
- High support cost per dollar of revenue
- Can't justify dedicated features
- Enterprise features don't translate

### The Amazon Analogy

Amazon started with books. Not because books were the biggest market, but because books were the best wedge — standardized products, infinite selection advantages, passionate early adopters. They used books to build the infrastructure that sold everything.

**Single-location restaurants are our books.** The infrastructure we build — voice capture, domain agents, workflow encoding — applies to any business where an operator is drowning in admin work.

## Why Big Players Won't Do This

### 1. Incentive Misalignment
Toast makes money on payment processing and software subscriptions. Voice-first operations doesn't drive transactions.

### 2. Organizational Impossibility
Building Mise required sitting with Jon for months learning his specific workflow. Toast has 120,000 restaurants. They can't do this.

### 3. Generic vs. Specific
Toast will build generic "voice notes." We have Papa Surf's specific tip pooling rules, DST-based shift schedules, name normalization, and Toast CSV integration.

### 4. Small Customers Don't Matter to Them
Jon is Toast's worst customer. Single location, minimal upsells. They don't build for him.

---

## The Moat (Why We Stay Ahead)

### 1. Workflow Specs as Code
Restaurant-specific logic lives in version-controlled specification files. Competitors would need to rebuild this architecture from scratch.

### 2. The Brain Grows with Usage
Every payroll Jon processes teaches the system. Name normalization gets better. Edge cases get documented. After a year, the brain knows things no competitor can learn without that same year of usage.

### 3. Workflow Lock-in
Once Jon's entire operational rhythm flows through Mise, switching means re-learning everything. Not because we trap him, but because the system has become HIS system.

### 4. Knowledge Compounds
Every week we operate, we learn new edge cases. A competitor starting later isn't competing with 2025 Mise — they're competing with 2027 Mise. They can never catch up by copying.
